# From France to Canada — .NET Developer in Montreal

In September 2024, I left Issy-les-Moulineaux to join **Desjardins**, one of the largest financial cooperatives in the world, in Montreal. Nine months later, here's what I took away from this professional and personal adventure.

## The (positive) culture shock

The first thing that strikes you when joining a Canadian team is the **feedback culture**. Meetings are structured differently: opinions are expressed openly, without the implicit hierarchy we're used to in France. Juniors and seniors speak as equals in meetings. It's disorienting at first, then very liberating.

Another notable difference: the punctuality of Agile ceremonies. Standups start and end on time. Period. What I had seen as a formality in France becomes a true discipline there.

## The technical mission

At Desjardins, I worked on a critical migration: moving application components from **Windows Server 2012 / SQL Server 2012** to a modern, secure infrastructure. The stakes were high: critical financial data, teams spread across multiple sites, zero tolerance for downtime.

What stood out most technically:

- Very mature use of **Azure DevOps** to track everything (tickets, pipelines, releases)
- Rigorous validation processes and **Change Management**
- The importance placed on **Knowledge Transfer** — every delivery comes with documentation for support teams

## What I learned about myself

This mission confirmed something important: I enjoy coordinating as much as coding. My role as Application Delivery Manager led me to orchestrate teams, facilitate deployment committees, and present strategies to non-technical stakeholders. It's a skill I now consciously develop.

## Practical tips

If you're considering a mission in Quebec:

- **Open a bank account right away** — Desjardins (cooperative, very accessible to newcomers) or RBC
- **Quebec technical French** constantly mixes French and English. You'll hear "le *build* a *failer*" or "on va *merger* la *branche*" in the same sentence
- **Allow 2-3 months to adapt** — the pace of life is different, winters are real, and people are extraordinarily welcoming

## In summary

Working in Canada gave me an international perspective that I now bring to every mission. If the opportunity arises again, I won't hesitate for a second.
